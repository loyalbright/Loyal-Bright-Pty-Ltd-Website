Individual Tax Return Checklist

Published on: August 15, 2024

Preparing for your individual tax return doesn't have to be stressful. Being organized and having the right documents ready ensures that we can maximize your eligible deductions and process your return efficiently.

Before your appointment with the Loyal Bright team, please gather the following information and documents applicable to your situation:

1. Income Records

  • Income Statements / Payment Summaries (PAYG): You usually do not need to bring these as we can access them directly via the ATO portal once your employer finalises them.
  • Government Payments: Details of any pensions, allowances, or JobSeeker payments.
  • Investment Income: Bank interest, dividend statements from shares, or managed fund tax statements.
  • Rental Property Income: Statements from your real estate agent detailing rental income and expenses.
  • Foreign Income: Details of any income earned from overseas.

2. Work-Related Deductions

Note: You must have spent the money yourself, it must be directly related to earning your income, and you must have a record to prove it.

  • Vehicle & Travel Expenses: Logbook records (if applicable) and receipts for work-related travel (excluding travel from home to work).
  • Uniforms & Protective Clothing: Receipts for purchasing, repairing, or cleaning occupation-specific clothing or protective gear.
  • Working from Home (WFH): A record of the hours you worked from home, plus receipts for home office equipment (laptops, desks, chairs) and internet/phone bills.
  • Self-Education: Receipts for courses or seminars directly related to your current job.
  • Tools & Equipment: Receipts for tools or software required for your work.

3. Other Deductions & Offsets

  • Charitable Donations: Receipts for donations made to registered Deductible Gift Recipients (DGRs).
  • Tax Agent Fees: The receipt for our accounting fees from last year (this is tax-deductible!).
  • Income Protection Insurance: Statements for premiums paid (if paid outside of your Superannuation).

4. General Information

  • Private Health Insurance: Your annual tax statement from your health fund.
