Pre-June 30 EOFY Tax Planning Guide

Updated: April 2026

Why act before June 30?

The Australian financial year ends on June 30. For Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs, typically facing a 25% base rate entity tax), implementing strategic tax planning before this deadline can significantly reduce your taxable profit, lowering your impending tax bill.

1. Pre-paying Expenses

If you are an eligible small business (turnover less than $50 million), one of the most straightforward ways to reduce tax is to pre-pay expenses. If you pay for upcoming business expenses before June 30 (for a service period not exceeding 12 months), you can claim the full tax deduction in this current financial year.

Common expenses to pre-pay include:

  • Next year's Business Insurance or Public Liability premiums.
  • Office or warehouse rent.
  • Annual software subscriptions (e.g., Xero, MYOB, Microsoft 365).
  • Professional association memberships or next year's accounting/audit fees.

2. Writing off Bad Debts

If you have invoiced a client but the amount remains unpaid and is deemed unrecoverable, you cannot simply leave it sitting in your accounts receivable. You must physically write it off as a bad debt in your accounting system before June 30.

Only by formally writing it off prior to the EOFY deadline can you claim the tax deduction for this financial year and potentially recover the GST you may have already paid to the ATO.

3. Deferring Income

If your business accounts on an Accruals Basis and you have a transaction or service nearing completion in late June, you might consider deferring the final delivery and invoicing until after July 1. By doing this, the income is recorded in the next financial year, effectively delaying the tax payable on that revenue by a full year.

Important Note: Tax planning must have a genuine commercial purpose, and relevant payments or accounting entries must be completed before June 30. Before making any significant pre-payments, please contact our team to ensure your cash flow remains healthy and compliant with ATO regulations.
